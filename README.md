# Food Nutrition Analysis (EDA)

## Overview

This project presents an exploratory data analysis (EDA) of a food nutrition dataset to investigate nutritional patterns, compare food categories, and identify relationships between different dietary components.

The analysis focuses on understanding how calories, proteins, fats, saturated fats, fiber, and carbohydrates are distributed across different food groups using various statistical and visualization techniques.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains nutritional information for a wide range of food items, including:

- Food name
- Food category
- Calories
- Protein
- Fat
- Saturated fat
- Fiber
- Carbohydrates

Before analysis, the dataset was cleaned by handling missing values, converting numerical features, and removing inconsistent entries.

---

## Objectives

The main goals of this project are:

- Explore the nutritional characteristics of different food categories.
- Identify foods with high levels of protein, calories, and fat.
- Analyze relationships between nutritional variables.
- Compare nutritional contributions among food groups.
- Create informative visualizations for better understanding of dietary patterns.

---

## Exploratory Data Analysis

The following analyses were performed:

### Nutrient Distribution Analysis
Kernel Density Estimation (KDE) plots were generated to explore relationships between:

- Carbohydrates and protein
- Fat and carbohydrates
- Fiber and fat
- Calories and carbohydrates
- Saturated fat and total fat

### Top Nutritional Sources

Food items were ranked based on:

- Highest protein content
- Highest calorie content
- Highest fat content

### Category-Based Analysis

Food categories were grouped and compared based on their total contribution to:

- Calories
- Protein
- Fat
- Fiber
- Saturated fat
- Carbohydrates

### Visualization Techniques

Multiple visualization methods were applied:

- Bar charts
- Pie charts
- Donut charts
- KDE plots
- Boxen plots
- Bubble charts
- 3D scatter plots

---

## Key Findings

- Bread, cereals, and grain-based products represent major contributors to carbohydrate and calorie intake.
- Seafood and meat categories contain some of the highest protein values.
- Fat-rich foods are mainly concentrated among animal fats, processed products, and high-fat ingredients.
- Fruits and vegetables generally contribute more to fiber content compared with other food groups.
- Nutritional characteristics vary significantly across food categories, highlighting differences in dietary composition.
